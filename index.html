Jump Rope Tricks

My reason for joining the fancy jump rope team is quite simple. One day, while leaving school, I saw the fancy jump rope team training and thought it looked really cool. I practiced hard at home and eventually succeeded in joining the team. Unfortunately, during a competition, I twisted my ankle, which led to a loss of interest in fancy jump rope. This was my first exposure to a sport, and it ignited my love for athletics, prompting me to continually seek out new sports to explore.

Table Tennis

I remember when I was in fourth grade, all my friends were playing table tennis, so I decided to join them in a table tennis interest class. This sparked my interest in the sport, and I joined the school table tennis team. Over the next three years, our team won two championships, and I was very happy because those achievements brought me a great sense of accomplishment. However, after moving to secondary school, I became too busy with my studies to continue training.

Basketball

Basketball has been a sport I loved from elementary school all the way through to my associate degree. I initially started playing basketball at a street court with friends, often playing from noon until the lights turned off. I remember being invited back to my elementary school after entering secondary school to participate in an alumni basketball game, where we won third place. That was not just an award; it was a recognition of my efforts. However, during my secondary school years, I didn’t join the school team because my parents wanted me to focus on my studies.

Secondary School Years

The main sports I participated in during my secondary school years included dodgeball, dragon boat racing, and athletics. I will explain each in detail below.

Below are the achievements and experiences I gained in various sports.
Dodgeball

Dodgeball was the first sport I encountered in secondary school. I was initially recommended to join the team by a teacher, and I soon developed an interest in the sport. Dodgeball accompanied me throughout my entire secondary school journey. I was on the school dodgeball team for five years and participated in two competitions, unfortunately losing both. However, I don’t believe dodgeball didn’t offer me anything; it brought me a group of like-minded friends, which is a significant achievement in itself.

Dragon boat

I discovered dragon boat racing in my sixth year. At the time, I was preparing for the DSE exams and felt a lot of pressure. I shared my feelings with a teacher, who suggested I join dragon boat training as a way to relieve stress. To my surprise, during my first competition representing the school, I led the team to win third place. I was extremely happy and never expected to achieve an award with such a short training period and no prior competition experience. This award was the greatest recognition of my efforts.

Athletics

The term "athletics" is quite broad, so I will only mention the two events I dedicated the most time to: the 100-meter sprint and javelin throw. I was very interested in both and spent a lot of time training. Unfortunately, during that time, the COVID-19 pandemic worsened, and since I hadn’t been vaccinated, I couldn't participate in school competitions or athletics meets. I only had the chance to compete in my sixth year’s final athletics meet. However, all those years of training paid off, as I ultimately won a gold medal in javelin and a bronze medal in the 100-meter sprint. When I received the medals on the podium, I cried because I had waited my entire secondary school life for this achievement, which was the greatest recognition of my hard work.
